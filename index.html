Intro

Education

The most valuable investment.
2012 – 2016

University of Cambridge

Doctor of Philosophy in Physics

Cambridge, UK

Thesis: "Antiferromagnets for Spintronics".
I found that antiferromagnets, which are materials that have "invisible" magnetic properties for the outside world, can be very efficient components for spintronic devices. Find more on my google scholar page and on the microelectronics group website. The latter, by the way, I developed myself.

2010 – 2012

ETH Zurich

Master of Science in Physics

Zurich, Switzerland

I believe the Physics MSc programme here is one of the best ones in the world. I took courses mainly focused on theoretical and experimental condensed matter physics. I also did a project in Prof. Ensslin's group working under the supervision of Dr. Peter Leek on coupling double quantum dots to microwave resonators. I did numerical simulations using Mathematica and built low pass filters.

2006 – 2010

Yerevan State University

Bachelor of Science in Physics

Yerevan, Armenia

My focus here was theoretical physics. I was in the special group of 12 students selected based on a test taken during the first week. Our lectures were separate from the rest of the course, which gave us more freedom to communicate with the professors and gain deeper understanding. My diploma thesis was on "Fermionic Casimir effect in compact spacetimes with applications to carbon nanotubes".
